# Source: US instruction manual (archive.org scan)

Reviewed 2026-07-06. Scanned official US PS2 manual, archive.org item [`ps2_Buzz_Junior-_Jungle_Party_USA`](https://archive.org/details/ps2_Buzz_Junior-_Jungle_Party_USA) (PDF + OCR text). Copyright Sony Computer Entertainment — facts paraphrased here; no scans stored in this repo. This is the project's **primary source** for menus, controls, and five official mini-game rule sets.

## Voice and framing

The manual is narrated in-character by the **gorilla of the jungle** — self-described as the strongest and smartest animal in the jungle — who welcomes the reader to a jungle that throws a party every day and has exactly one rule: you must have fun. This narrator persona matches the [announcer tone](../presentation.md#announcer) and the gorilla NPC who appears in mini-games (he is the dozing gorilla in [Bubble Bath](../bubble-bath.md)).

## Hardware and controls

- Buzz! Buzzers connect via a single USB connector into either front USB port; plug in before power-on. See [buzz-controller](../buzz-controller.md).
- Control diagram labels five buttons: the **Buzz! button** (the big red one) plus **blue, orange, green, yellow** buttons.
- Universal menu convention: **blue/orange scroll, Buzz! confirms**. Used for language select at first boot, name entry (scroll letters, Buzz! picks; Delete/Done entries), leaderboard select, custom-list building.

## Menus and modes (as documented)

- **Main menu**: blue = Multiplayer, orange = Single Player, green = Practice, yellow = Options.
- **Multiplayer** (up to four players): first choose length — blue Short / orange Medium / green Long / yellow Marathon — then blue Quick Start / orange Standard Game / green Custom Game.
  - *Quick Start*: each player presses a colour button to claim that monkey; when all four have chosen, the games begin.
  - *Standard Game*: choose monkey by seated position, then per-player name entry and costume select.
  - *Custom Game*: build a saved playlist of favourite mini-games (blue/orange scroll the game list, Buzz! adds; name the list; save to memory card slot 1 or 2), or play a previously saved list.
- **Costume select**: blue/orange highlight the monkey's head, body, or legs; green/yellow scroll the items for that slot; Buzz! accepts. See [monkeys](../monkeys.md).
- **Single Player**: choose a monkey colour (blue/orange scroll + Buzz!), enter name, pick costume, then play for a total score aiming at the **Jungle Party Hall of Fame** leaderboard.
- **Practice**: blue = Multiplayer practice, orange = Single Player practice; then scroll and pick any mini-game.
- **Options**: blue Save/Load (autosave toggle, load data, delete character), orange Screen (alignment moved with the four colour buttons, widescreen toggle), green replay the intro movie, yellow view leaderboards (Single Player and Multiplayer).
- **Pause**: hold **all four colour buttons at once** during a mini-game; blue resumes, orange quits to the Title Screen.
- Every mini-game is preceded by an explanation; the **Buzz! button skips it**. (See [game-structure](../game-structure.md).)

## Official mini-game rules (the five documented in the manual)

- **[Bubble Bath](../bubble-bath.md)** — monkeys share a bath with the dozing gorilla narrator. *Hold* the Buzz! button to make bubbles; release before the gorilla wakes or he hits the offender on the head.
- **[Jungle Hurdles](../jungle-hurdles.md)** — a running race with coloured obstacles: press the button matching each hurdle's colour (blue button for a blue hurdle, etc.), and press Buzz! to avoid banana skins on the track or slip over.
- **Keep It Up** — keepy-uppy: Buzz! throws the ball up, Buzz! again with timing keeps it airborne; the longer you wait to hit it, the more points; Buzz! relaunches after a miss. (Not in the starter six; catalog entry in [minigames](../minigames.md).)
- **Island War** — hurl coconuts at anything that moves: a red arrow sweeps left–right in front of your monkey; press Buzz! to lock the direction (the arrow then grows in length), press Buzz! again to throw; the arrow's length at release sets the throw distance. (Catalog entry in [minigames](../minigames.md).)
- **[Monkey Bomb](../monkey-bomb.md)** — a bomb with an unknown fuse is passed among the monkeys: press the colour button of the monkey you want to throw it to (green button → green monkey); the longer you hold it the more points you earn; whoever holds it at the explosion loses that round's points **and 100 points from their total**.
