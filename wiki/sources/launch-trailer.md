## Art direction observations

- **The monkeys** ([monkeys](../monkeys.md)): small chimp-like characters with oversized heads and ears, big glossy round eyes, pale cream faces/muzzles with a heavy brow, and body fur tinted in the four player colours (blue, orange, green, yellow) topped with a swept hair tuft. The cinematic CG models are soft, plush and appealing; the in-game PSP models are noticeably chunkier and lower-poly, and wear costumes per mini-game (the blue monkey's skull-print hoodie and red sneakers recur, plus crash helmets in Rocket Riders and cloth caps in the HUD portraits).
- **Palette**: saturated tropical greens dominate (dense foliage, painted leaf-collage backdrops), set against turquoise lagoon water, warm brown rock and wood, and the golden-orange/red logo typography. HUD elements are colour-coded per player in bright blue/orange/green/yellow nameplate bars.
- **Lighting/camera**: the CG bookends use warm sunbeams and volumetric haze through the canopy; gameplay clips use fixed or gently framed cameras typical of party-game staging (top-down for the pond game, side-on for lineup games, low chase angle for penalties). One scene (character select, penalties) shifts to a cooler night-time mood.
- **UI**: PlayStation face-button iconography is everywhere — placards, curtains, totem blocks, floating prompts — plus a recurring leaf-shaped timer dial and boxed score counters. Title cards all share the leaf-wall background and the logo's chunky drop-shadow typeface.
- **Pacing/energy**: a slower, storybook CG open (about 9 seconds) snaps into a rapid-fire montage of roughly 2-second gameplay clips, then decompresses again for the banana-pile celebration, COMING SOON card and hardware/legal end cards. The overall energy is bouncy, slapstick and relentlessly colourful — pure button-basher marketing. → [presentation](../presentation.md).
