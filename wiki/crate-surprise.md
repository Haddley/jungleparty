# Crate Surprise

**Category: Luck** · **Controls: colour buttons** · Longplay chapter 0:14:48 ([watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZEJTdSRqrY&t=888s)). Not yet recreated; rules *(inferred)*, with one likely trailer sighting.

A pick-a-box luck game: choose a crate and take what's inside — randomly good or bad *(inferred)*.

## What the sources say

- [2009 Wikipedia list](sources/wikipedia.md) for the name; German Wikipedia for the **Luck** category.
- The [launch trailer](sources/launch-trailer.md) (0:38–0:39) shows a scene *likely* from this game: monkeys **flattened on the grass beside fallen anvils**, with button-symbol banners hanging from ropes overhead — suggesting the "surprise" can be a falling anvil, and that choices are made on button prompts. The identification is not confident (noted as "likely Crate Surprise" in the trailer notes).

## Rules *(inferred)*

- Each round presents four colour-marked crates (or hanging banners); every player picks one on their colour buttons.
- Contents are random: points/bananas for lucky picks, an anvil (or similar slapstick) for unlucky ones — the flattened-monkey pose in the trailer matches the [monkeys](monkeys.md) `flattened` animation state.
- Several rounds; highest total wins. Whether multiple players may pick the same crate is unknown.

## Verification targets for a video pass

Number of crates vs. players, shared or exclusive picks, reward/penalty values, whether any information lets players deduce a safe crate (pure luck vs. light deduction).

## Recreation sketch (phone-buzzer fit: excellent)

- Four colour crates on the TV, pick simultaneously on phones within a countdown; dramatic staggered reveal.
- Slapstick payoff reuses our rig: `flat` pose + dust puffs for anvil picks, `cheer` + confetti for jackpots.
- Parameter sketch: 6 rounds; one anvil (−100), one jackpot (+300), two middling (+50/+100) per round, shuffled; simultaneous same-crate picks allowed and share the fate.
