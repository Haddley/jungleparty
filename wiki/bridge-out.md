# Bridge Out

**Category: Accuracy** · **Controls: Buzz! button** · Longplay chapter 0:03:00 ([watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZEJTdSRqrY&t=180s)). Not yet recreated; rules *(inferred)* pending a video pass.

Bridge warfare: knock rival monkeys' bridge sections away with lobbed coconuts *(inferred)* — reconstructed from the name, the Accuracy category, and the game's recurring coconut-artillery motif (see [Island War](island-war.md), [Lethal Lily Pads](lethal-lily-pads.md), [Up The Creek](up-the-creek.md)).

## What the sources say

- [2009 Wikipedia list](sources/wikipedia.md) for the name; German Wikipedia for the **Accuracy** category.
- The launch trailer's CG intro shows monkeys scampering across **two rope bridges over a turquoise lagoon** ([trailer notes](sources/launch-trailer.md) 0:11–0:19) — likely the setting this game borrows, though that shot is cinematic, not gameplay.

## Rules *(inferred)*

- Each monkey stands on (or defends) a rope-bridge of destructible sections over water.
- Buzz! presses lob coconuts — probably via the series' sweep-and-release aiming (lock direction, then power), as documented for [Island War](island-war.md) in the manual.
- Losing all your sections (or falling) eliminates or penalises you; last monkey standing or most sections destroyed wins.

## Verification targets for a video pass

Aiming mechanic (sweep vs. timing), whether players target bridges or monkeys, elimination vs. score, round length.

## Recreation sketch (phone-buzzer fit: good)

- Same one-button sweep-aim artillery core as our Island War sketch; bridges give it stakes and a visible health bar (sections remaining).
- Host renders bridges as segment arrays; a hit removes the nearest segment; monkey plays the `bonked`/`flat` rig pose when its section drops.
- Parameter sketch: 5 sections each, ~60 s cap, throw cycle ~3 s, sudden-death wobble when one section left.
