# Card Counting

**Category: Speed** · **Controls: colour buttons** · Longplay chapter 0:12:42 ([watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZEJTdSRqrY&t=762s)). Not yet recreated; rules reported by a detailed review plus a trailer sighting.

Fastest-finger arithmetic: cards showing fruit counts are revealed, and the quickest player to press the colour of the highest-count card scores.

## What the sources say

- [Wilcox Arcade](sources/reviews.md) documents the rules — cards show coconut counts; fastest to press the colour of the highest-count card wins the round.
- The [launch trailer](sources/launch-trailer.md) (0:44–0:45, PSP version) shows monkeys clinging to vines while **oversized playing cards covered in strawberries** flip in mid-frame, each player answering with an assigned face button. Fruit differs from Wilcox's coconuts — either version variation or multiple fruit types; flag for the video pass.
- German Wikipedia category: **Speed**.

## Rules (reported / *(inferred)* where noted)

- Each round reveals a set of cards (one per colour) bearing a count of items.
- All players race to press the colour of the card with the **most** items; fastest correct answer scores *(points value unverified)*.
- Wrong answers presumably lock you out or score nothing for the round *(inferred)*.
- Several quick rounds per game *(inferred)*, standard for the fastest-finger family.

## Verification targets for a video pass

Card count range (how hard the counting is), points per round, wrong-answer penalty, number of rounds, whether ties/simultaneous presses occur.

## Recreation sketch (phone-buzzer fit: excellent)

- Pure colour-button reflex — direct port. TV renders four cards colour-framed per player colour; all four colour buttons enabled during the answer window.
- Difficulty knob: counts within ±1 of each other (hard) vs. spread (easy); ramp across rounds.
- Parameter sketch: 8 rounds; reveal → 4 s answer window; +100 fastest correct, +50 later correct, wrong = 1 s lockout (mirrors our [Whack A Squirrel](whack-a-squirrel.md) whiff rule).
