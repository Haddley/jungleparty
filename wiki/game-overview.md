# Game overview

**Buzz! Junior: Jungle Party** is a 2006 PlayStation 2 party game — the first of the Buzz! Junior spin-off series, which replaces the mainline Buzz! quiz format with pure reflex mini-games for young children while keeping the four-player [Buzz! buzzer controllers](buzz-controller.md). Players are four cheeky [monkeys](monkeys.md) competing across dozens of jungle-set mini-games; the announcer keeps score and the best monkey becomes King of the Jungle. ([Wikipedia](sources/wikipedia.md))

- **Developer**: Magenta Software (Liverpool, UK); PS3 port by Cohort Studios. **Publisher**: Sony Computer Entertainment (SCEE / SCEA). Intro CG movie by RealtimeUK. ([manual credits](sources/us-instruction-manual.md#credits-selected))
- **Releases and content**: PS2 EU 20 Oct 2006 (40 multiplayer + 10 single-player games); PS2 NA 30 Oct 2007 (25 games); PS3 PSN 2008–09 (5 games, 720p); PSP "Jungle Party" 5 Nov 2010, EU/AU (15 games). The [launch trailer we ingested](sources/launch-trailer.md) is for the 2010 PSN/PSP version.
- **Audience/rating**: PEGI 3 / ESRB E10+ ("Crude Humor, Mild Cartoon Violence" — think bonks, zaps, and bath bubbles). Every game is learnable in under a minute. ([reviews](sources/reviews.md))
- **Reception**: Metacritic 70; won the **BAFTA British Academy Children's Award 2007** for video game.

## Why it matters for this project

Our recreation targets the game's core loop — four players with one-red-plus-four-colour-button controllers playing short jungle mini-games for [banana standings](game-structure.md#session-scoring) — with phones as buzzers. Scope and design decisions live in [recreation-notes](recreation-notes.md); the full game catalog is in [minigames](minigames.md).
