# Odd One Out

**Category: Speed** · **Controls: colour buttons** · Longplay chapter 1:11:33 ([watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZEJTdSRqrY&t=4293s)). Not yet recreated; core rule from the catalog plus a vivid trailer sighting.

Spot the misfit: four items are shown, and the fastest player to press the colour of the one that doesn't belong scores. Also on the ten-game [single-player list](minigames.md#single-player-exclusives), and one of two games with a known **beta instruction video** (with [Door Running](door-running.md)) per the [instruction-videos source](sources/instruction-videos.md).

## What the sources say

- [Catalog](minigames.md): "Four items shown; fastest to press the colour of the one that doesn't belong."

Costumed hippos are shared with [Hippo Splash](hippo-splash.md).
- German Wikipedia category: **Speed**.

## Rules (reported / *(inferred)* where noted)

- Each round reveals four colour-slotted subjects; three match, one differs.
- Fastest correct colour press scores; wrong presses presumably lock out or forfeit the round *(inferred)*.
- The trailer's example is a *loud* difference; later rounds presumably get subtler *(inferred — standard quiz ramp, cf. Hippo Splash)*.

## Verification targets for a video pass

Subject variety (hippos only, or rotating casts?), reveal cadence, scoring value, wrong-press penalty, difficulty ramp, differences between beta and shipped instruction videos.
