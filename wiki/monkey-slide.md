# Monkey Slide

**Category: Luck & tactics** · **Controls: colour buttons** · Longplay chapter 1:07:43 ([watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZEJTdSRqrY&t=4063s)). Not yet recreated; rules *(partly inferred)*.

A queue-of-doom game: monkeys slide along a branch toward something painful, and jumping to the **back of the queue** is how you avoid being the one who hits the end *(partly inferred)*. The third member of the Luck & tactics family with [Monkey Bomb](monkey-bomb.md) and [Freefall](freefall.md) — here the "hot potato" is your **position** rather than an object.

- [2009 Wikipedia list](sources/wikipedia.md) for the name; German Wikipedia for the **Luck & tactics** category; colour-button controls from the catalog.

## Rules *(partly inferred)*

- All four monkeys slide in single file toward a hazard (the end of the branch, a cactus, a mouth — unverified).
- Some action (press timing? a colour choice? cost?) moves you from the front to the back of the line; the front monkey when the slide ends takes the hit.
- Colour buttons suggest you may choose *whom* to swap with rather than simply jumping back *(speculative — would explain the "tactics" tag)*.

## Verification targets for a video pass

What the hazard is, the exact jump/swap input and any cost or cooldown, whether the slide speed ramps, points structure (survival vs. score).

## Recreation sketch (phone-buzzer fit: good)

- Model as position-based hot potato: front slot accrues danger, a limited stock of "jump" tokens (say 3 per player per slide) sends you to the back — spend early and you're helpless late.
- Colour-button variant per the tactics reading: press an opponent's colour to swap with them, each player having 2 swaps per slide; the mind-games write themselves.
- Parameter sketch: 5 slides of ~12 s; end-of-branch victim −100 and `bonked`; survivors +100 each; queue rendered on a long springy branch using the scenery vocabulary.
