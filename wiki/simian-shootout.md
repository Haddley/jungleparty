# Simian Shootout

Category: **Speed**. Controls: the **red Buzz! button** only. A fastest-finger reaction game dressed up as a western quick-draw: two [monkeys](monkeys.md) face off in front of a gorilla referee, wait through his teasing, and the first to hit the red button when he raises his big red buzzer shoots the other with a banana pistol and takes the points. Draw too soon and the round is handed to your opponent. One of the games in the [minigames](minigames.md) catalog; general reception of the minigame set is covered in [reviews](sources/reviews.md).

Sources: all observations below are from the [longplay](sources/full-longplay.md), chapter 1:31:38 ([watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZEJTdSRqrY&t=5498s)) — an instruction video followed by one full 3-player game (Blue, Orange, Green; no Yellow this session). Clip timestamps below are relative to the chapter start.

## Structure & scoring as observed

- **Duels are strictly one-on-one.** Only two monkeys are ever on screen; the sitting-out player's monkey is simply absent. The session ran as a **round-robin of pairings**: Blue vs Orange, then Orange vs Green, then Blue vs Green.
- **Each pairing plays three duels**, so this 3-player game was **9 duels total** (~0:42 to ~3:20 of the chapter, about 2 min 40 s of play). The 4-player structure was not observed.
- A new pairing opens with the words **"FIRST ROUND"** in large spaced letters scrolling across mid-screen (seen at 0:42 and again at 1:33 when the second pairing began — the round count restarts per pairing). Later duels in a pairing start without visible text in the sampled frames.
- **Winner of a duel: +1000 points**, credited with the standard rolling odometer count-up (mid-roll values 0972 and 0999 are readable). No other point value was ever awarded and no score ever decreased.
- **A small tick/checkmark pip appears under a player's score box for each duel won**, accumulating through the game (Orange showed two pips at 2000).
- On a fast winning press the word **"PERFECT"** slides along the ground under the duelists (observed on at least two wins). The payout was still 1000, so the rating appears cosmetic — whether slower wins get a different word is not confirmed by this clip.
- **False start (observed, duel 1, ~0:46):** Blue drew and fired while the gorilla was still in his waiting pose. The banana blast still knocked Orange head-over-heels — but the gorilla scolded Blue, pointing and wagging at him while Blue facepalmed and his banana drooped limply, and the **1000 points were awarded to Orange**, flat on his back and all. An early press is thus an automatic loss of the duel, not merely ignored.
- Final scores: **Orange 4000, Blue 3000, Green 2000** (every one of the 9 duels paid out). Highest total wins; Orange's monkey got the closing celebration.

## Scene

A grassy jungle clearing in front of a raised **bamboo hut/veranda** with wooden steps, framed by bamboo posts, hanging vines and ferns; butterflies flit through the background. The two duelists stand on small grass mounds at screen left and right, side-on to each other at classic paces-apart distance, with the **referee — a huge pot-bellied gorilla in a black coat** — planted centre on the steps between them.

The staging is pure spaghetti-western pastiche: the monkeys wear **big cowboy hats** (a yellow ten-gallon on the left duelist, an enormous floppy white/grey sombrero on the right — hats stay with the podium side, fur colour identifies the player), plus **belts with a banana holstered** at the hip. During the wait they hold a hunched **gunslinger crouch, fingers twitching over the holster**. The monkeys "shoot" with the banana as a pistol. Before play the camera does slow dramatic close-ups of each hatted monkey's face, squinting under the brim.

## The signal

- The gorilla referee holds the game's centrepiece prop: a **giant lollipop-style buzzer — a red ball/button on a yellow paddle handle**, an oversized stand-in for the Buzz! controller's red button.
- **Wait phase:** he keeps the buzzer low at his chest, idly fiddling with it or giving it a lick like an ice lolly, while his other hand holds up a single index finger — "wait for it". This tease runs roughly **3–5 seconds** per duel and the length varies.
- **The signal:** he **thrusts the red buzzer high above his head, presenting the red ball to the camera** — press the red Buzz! button now. On the first duel the raise came as an arms-flung-wide open-mouthed roar; on later duels it was a cleaner one-arm raise. The roar variant is also the moment Blue jumped early, so the flamboyant wind-up plausibly doubles as a bait/fake-out; the footage is not conclusive on deliberate fake signals.
- After the winning shot the gorilla keeps the buzzer aloft through the "PERFECT"/tally beat, then settles back to his idle (buzzer resting in his lap) between duels.

## Win/lose animations

- **Winning draw:** the monkey whips the banana from its belt, lunges into a low shooting stance with arm fully extended and fires; a yellow burst appears on the victim.
- **Losing:** the shot monkey is **blasted backwards head-over-heels**, sometimes clean into the bamboo at the screen edge, and ends **sprawled flat on its back** (hat knocked askew or off) where it stays through the score tally.
- **False-start aftermath:** gorilla points and wags a finger at the offender; the offender slumps, facepalms, and its banana visibly droops.
- **Between duels:** loser picks itself up and both return to the crouch; the gorilla laughs at good knockdowns.
- **Game end (~3:19):** the champion (Orange) bounces with both arms raised in front of the applauding gorilla, then the leaf-curtain transition wipes the scene.

## HUD

One panel per active player across the top (three this session): monkey face in a colour-ringed badge, colour name on a coloured banner (BLUE / ORANGE / GREEN), and the four-digit **odometer-style score box**. Under the score box, small **red tick pips** accumulate one per duel won. Both duelists' and the resting player's panels stay on screen throughout. The instruction video plays in the usual bamboo-framed screen with a wooden "SIMIAN SHOOTOUT" nameplate over the leaf backdrop.

## Recreation notes

Parameters for a faithful remake, from the observed session:

- **Players per duel:** exactly 2; others sit out. With N=3 players: round-robin of all 3 pairings.
- **Duels per pairing:** 3. Total duels (3P): 9. Whole game ≈ 160 s; one duel cycle ≈ 10–18 s (announce → tease → draw → knockdown → tally).
- **Pairing intro:** "FIRST ROUND" letters scroll across mid-screen (~2 s); camera close-ups of the duelists before the first duel of the game.
- **Wait time:** randomized ≈ 3–5 s of gorilla tease (buzzer at chest, finger raised) before the signal. Vary it — the false start in the footage shows the anticipation works.
- **Signal:** gorilla raises the red lollipop buzzer overhead (occasionally with an arms-wide roar). Input = red Buzz! button only, first valid press wins.
- **Points:** +1000 to the duel winner, rolling count-up; win pip under the score. Fast wins flash "PERFECT" along the ground.
- **False start:** pressing before the signal immediately ends the duel and awards the +1000 to the opponent; play offender-scold animations (finger wag, facepalm, drooping banana). The early shot still knocks the opponent over — pure theatre.
- **End:** highest total wins; winner celebration with gorilla applause, leaf-curtain out.
- **Unknowns:** 4-player pairing order/count; whether slower wins show a rating other than "PERFECT"; whether both players false-starting is handled specially; exact wait-time distribution.
