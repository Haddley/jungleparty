# Campfire Chaos

**Category: Memory** · **Controls: colour buttons** · Longplay chapter 0:07:34 ([watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZEJTdSRqrY&t=454s)). Not yet recreated; core rule reported by a review, details pending a video pass.

A night-time memory game at a campfire: watch a colour sequence, then answer questions about which colours appeared. One of the few **Memory**-category games (with [Hippo Splash](hippo-splash.md)), and a natural fit for the night variant of our stage art.

## Rules (reported / *(inferred)* where noted)

- A sequence of coloured items (sparks, fireworks, or dancing figures around the fire *(inferred)*) plays out while players watch.
- Players are then quizzed — e.g. "which colour appeared?" or "which colour did NOT appear?" *(exact question format inferred)* — and answer on the colour buttons.
- Speed likely matters within correctness (fastest correct answer scores most) *(inferred)*, consistent with the game's other fastest-finger rounds like [Card Counting](card-counting.md) and [Odd One Out](odd-one-out.md).

## Recreation sketch (phone-buzzer fit: excellent)

- Purely colour-button driven — zero aiming — so it translates to phones with no compromise.
- Host shows the sequence on the TV (phones' buttons disabled during the watch phase — our `buttons` enable-map already supports this), then enables all four colours for the answer window.
- Parameter sketch: 6 rounds; sequence of 4–7 coloured sparks at 0.8 s intervals; answer window 5 s; +100 fastest correct, +50 other correct, 0 wrong.
- Would use the `night` stage flag (glow mushrooms, fireflies) plus a new campfire prop.
