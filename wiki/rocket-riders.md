# Rocket Riders

**Category: Skill** · **Controls: colour buttons** · Longplay chapter 1:24:23 ([watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZEJTdSRqrY&t=5063s)). Not yet recreated; rules from a detailed review plus a trailer sighting.

Sequence-entry under pressure: coloured button sequences appear, and entering yours fastest **fuels your rocket** — first to launch (and fly farthest?) wins.

## What the sources say

- [Wilcox Arcade](sources/reviews.md) documents the rules: enter the displayed colour sequences fast to fuel your rocket; first to launch/fly wins.
- The [launch trailer](sources/launch-trailer.md) (1:00–1:01, PSP) shows the staging: **helmeted monkeys perched atop firework-mortar cylinders ringed with button-symbol bands, fuses trailing**, in a village clearing — so the "rockets" are big comedy fireworks and the sequences are printed on the rocket bands.
- German Wikipedia category: **Skill**.

## Rules (reported / *(inferred)* where noted)

- Each player is shown colour sequences (per-player or shared is unverified); correctly keying a sequence adds fuel.
- Mistakes presumably reset the current sequence or cost time *(inferred)*.
- Filling the tank launches the firework with the monkey aboard; the win may be first-to-launch or flight distance after launch ("launch/fly" ambiguity in the review).

## Verification targets for a video pass

Sequence length/ramp, shared vs. individual sequences, error penalty, what happens after launch (does flight continue scoring?), round cap.

## Recreation sketch (phone-buzzer fit: excellent)

- Sequence-keying on four colour buttons is a perfect phone mechanic (private, latency-tolerant, no timing windows). TV shows each player's current sequence above their rocket; phone prompt mirrors the next required colour.
- Error handling: wrong press flashes and restarts that sequence only (Wilcox implies speed pressure, not harsh punishment).
- Parameter sketch: sequences of 3→6 colours as fuel fills; 8 fills to launch; first launch +1000, others by fuel; launch spectacle = fireworks canvas + `zap`-pose soot gag for a misfiring rocket (comedy beat, no score effect).
